# Star Wars: Age of Rebellion - GM Lore

## Blasters, Bowcasters, and Bad Armor
The blaster is everywhere: the rebel's battered DL-44 heavy pistol, the stormtrooper's E-11 rifle, the heavy repeating blasters that lock down corridors. Bolts are visible, survivable behind cover, and 'stun' is always an option - set for stun is how heroes keep a story bright. Stormtrooper armor turns glancing bolts and shrapnel but a clean hit drops them, which is why rebels joke about it and still fear the white tide. Thermal detonators are for desperate hours. A lightsaber is a legend most people have never seen; the Force, in these years, is a half-remembered religion that some old soldiers still swear by.

## Ships and Hyperspace
A light freighter is home, getaway car, and gun platform: hidden compartments for cargo and people, a topside turret, and a hyperdrive that fails exactly when the plot demands. Hyperspace travel needs calculated jumps along known lanes - jump wrong and you fly through a star. Interdiction means an Imperial cruiser yanking you back to realspace. TIE fighters are fast, fragile, and never alone; a Star Destroyer is a city-sized argument you do not win, only escape. Docking bays, fuel, and repair parts run on credits and favors, mostly favors.

## The Empire in the Outer Rim
Imperial rule on a Rim world is checkpoints, dock inspections, code cylinders, and the slow strangle of permits. The locals' loyalty is bought, frightened, or absent; informers earn bounties and neighbors learn to speak carefully. An Imperial garrison runs on routine - shift changes, patrol routes, requisition schedules - and routine is what a clever cell exploits. Above the routine sit the ISB and the Moffs, who answer failure with reassignment to somewhere worse, which makes mid-rank officers dangerous: they need a win.

## The Rebellion
The Alliance is cells that mostly never meet: a code phrase on the holonet, a contact in a cantina, supplies left in a crater. Secrecy is survival - what a captured rebel doesn't know, the interrogation droids can't take. Recruits come from everywhere: farm kids, deserters, droids wiped one too many times, smugglers who got tired of choosing sides. Money is short, ships are older than their pilots, and hope is the only thing in surplus. The unwritten rule of every cell: nobody gets left behind, and the mission comes first, and everyone lies about which rule wins.
